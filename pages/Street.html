Synopsis:

The game follows a worldwide martial arts tournament organized by the evil dictator M. Bison, leader of the criminal organization Shadaloo. Players choose from eight unique fighters, each with distinct moves and fighting styles, including: Ryu – A disciplined martial artist seeking self-improvement. Ken – Ryu’s American rival with similar skills. Chun-Li – An Interpol officer seeking revenge against M. Bison. Guile – A U.S. Air Force soldier avenging his fallen comrade. Zangief – A Russian wrestler fighting for his country. Dhalsim – A yoga master with stretchy limbs and fire attacks. E. Honda – A sumo wrestler proving the strength of his sport. Blanka – A beast-like fighter raised in the wild. Players fight through one-on-one battles, using special moves, combos, and counters to defeat opponents. The game introduced six-button controls, command-based special moves (like Hadouken and Shoryuken), and multiplayer versus mode, making it a cornerstone of c ompetitive gaming. Street Fighter II spawned multiple updates (Champion Edition, Turbo, Super), solidifying its place as one of the most influential video games ever.
